Empanadas

Description

This beef empanada recipe is made with Goya Discos pastry rounds and a mouthwatering filling of seasoned ground beef, tomatoes, onions, and garlic. Enjoy these flaky, deep-fried empanadas as an appetizer, a main dish, or even after your main course.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup black olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 packages pre-made empanada dough
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and garlic. Saut√© until they become translucent.
  3. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned.
  4. Stir in the green and red bell peppers, black olives, and raisins. Cook for a few minutes until the peppers soften.
  5. Next, add the tomato sauce, ground cumin, paprika, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well and let it simmer for a few more minutes.
  6. Preheat the oven to the temperature recommended on the empanada dough package.
  7. Roll out the empanada dough and cut it into rounds using a cookie cutter or a small plate as a guide.
  8. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough round.
  9. Brush the edges of the dough with water to help seal the empanadas.
  10. Fold the dough over the filling and press the edges together with a fork to create a tight seal.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.
  12. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  13. Brush the tops of the empanadas with beaten egg to give them a golden color when baked.
  14. Bake in the preheated oven for the time indicated on the empanada dough package or until they are golden brown and crispy.
  15. Remove from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving.
  16. Enjoy your delicious empanadas!
