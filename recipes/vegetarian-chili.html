Vegetarian Chili

Description

Vegetarian chili is a delightful meat-free version of classic chili, packed with colorful veggies, beans, and spices. It's hearty, nutritious, and bursting with flavor. The combination of red and green bell peppers, zucchini, carrots, and celery adds a rich texture, while spices like chili powder, cumin, and paprika infuse warmth and depth. The optional jalapeno brings a kick of heat for spice enthusiasts. Simmered in a tomato-based broth, the chili becomes a harmonious blend of flavors. Whether served as a comforting dinner, game-day favorite, or potluck dish, this versatile chili is sure to impress. It can be enjoyed on its own or topped with fresh cilantro, grated cheddar cheese, and sour cream for added indulgence. Easy to prepare and highly customizable, this wholesome chili provides a balanced and satisfying meal option. Embrace the warmth and comfort of this mouthwatering vegetarian chili, and savor the deliciousness of a classic dish with a plant-based twist.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 jalapeno, seeds removed and finely chopped (optional for heat)
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 2 cans (15 ounces each) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish (optional)
  • Grated cheddar cheese, for topping (optional)
  • Sour cream, for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they become translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic, chopped red and green bell peppers, and the optional jalapeno. Cook for a few minutes until the peppers soften.
  4. Stir in the diced carrots, celery, and zucchini. Cook for another 5 minutes to soften the vegetables.
  5. Add the corn, kidney beans, crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir everything together.
  6. Season the chili with chili powder, ground cumin, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasonings to your taste preference.
  7. Bring the chili to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot with a lid.
  8. Let the chili simmer for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together, stirring occasionally.
  9. Taste and adjust the seasonings if needed.
  10. Serve the vegetarian chili hot, garnished with chopped cilantro if desired.
  11. You can also top each serving with grated cheddar cheese and a dollop of sour cream for added creaminess and flavor.
  12. Enjoy your hearty and delicious vegetarian chili!
